Day 1

Shwezigon Pagoda

The Shwezigon Pagoda or Shwezigon Paya is a Buddhist stupa located in Nyaung-U, Myanmar. A prototype of Burmese stupas, it consists of a circular gold leaf-gilded stupa surrounded by smaller temples and shrines.

  • Location : Northwest of Kyanzittha Umin near the bank of Ayeyarwaddy River
  • Region : Nyaung U & Wetkyi-In
  • Built by : King Anawrahta
  • Date : A.D 1076
  • Height (max): 48.8 m (160 ft)

Wetkyi-in Gubyaukgyi Temple

The Gubyaukgyi temple, located just south of Bagan, Myanmar, in Myinkaba Village, is a Buddhist temple built in 1113 AD by Prince Yazakumar, shortly after the death of his father, King Kyansittha of the Pagan Dynasty.

  • Location : North of Myinkaba Paya
  • Region : Myinkaba
  • Built by : Prince Rajakumar
  • Date : A.D 1113
  • Monument Number : 1323

Htilominlo Temple

Situated close to the road between Nyaung U and Bagan, and about 1.5 km northeast of Bagan. This large temple was built by King Nantaungmya in 1218. The temple is known to be the last Myanmar Style temple built in Bagan

  • Location : South of Bagan Nyaung-U Main Road
  • Region : Old Bagan
  • Built by : King Nadaungmya @ King Zeya Theinkha
  • Date : A.D 1218

Day 2

Manuha Temple

The name "Manuha" was given after the Mon king from Thaton who was held captive in Bagan by King Anawrahta. Legend says that Manuha was allowed to build this temple in 1059, and that he constructed it to represent his displeasure at captivity.

  • Location : Northeast of Nanpaya
  • Region : Myinkaba
  • Built by : King Manuha
  • Date : A.D 1059

Bupaya

Bupaya means the "a gourd shape pagoda". The legend says, the third king of Bagan, Pyusawhti (AD 162-243), got rid of the gourd-like climbing plant "bu" that infested the riverbanks, before becoming the king.

  • Location : On the eastern bank of Ayeyarwaddy River
  • Region : Old Bagan
  • Built by : King Pyusawhti
  • Date : A.D 162

Balloons Over Bagan

Watch the sun rise over the many temples of Bagan on a hot air balloon flight.

  • Address: Balloons Over Bagan Sales Office Near Hotel Zfreeti, Thiripyitsaya Block No. (5, Nyaung-U)
  • Hours: Open - Monday to Sunday at 9AM - 8PM

Day 3

Maount Popa

Mount Popa is an extinct volcano 1518 metres above sea level, and located in central Myanmar in the region of Mandalay about 50 km southeast of Bagan in the Pegu Range. It can be seen from the Ayeyarwady River as far away as 60 km in clear weather.

  • Elevation: 1,518 m
  • Prominence: 1,150 m
  • Last eruption: 6050 BCE
  • Easiest route: Hiking
